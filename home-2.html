From Sand to Soul

1. Melting

Silica sand, soda ash, and lime are melted in a furnace at 2,400Â°F until they become molten glass.

2. Blowing

Using a blowpipe, the artist gathers glass and carefully blows air to create the initial bubble form.

3. Shaping

Tools like jacks and shears are used to shape the molten glass while constant rotation maintains symmetry.

4. Annealing

The piece is slowly cooled in a kiln over several hours or days to prevent cracking and ensure durability.
